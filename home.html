This is me

Hey Basecamp,

Do you have a CrossFitting, yoga practicing, (decaf) espresso drinking, ice cream loving, toddler wrangling designer on your team yet? You should!

I’m Courtney. I spend my days designing for ReCollect, working out, and chasing a toddler. I feel like I was made for this role. Like all the weird twists and turns of my career were leading me to Basecamp.

What I Do

Currently I am the one and only product designer at ReCollect. As a team (or manager) of one, I am a self-directed leader who gets sh*t done.

  • I lead all design-based projects at ReCollect.
  • I advocate for design improvements in the product roadmap.
  • I collaborate with Software Developers and Product Managers to come up with designs that are technically feasible and elegant, and solve real customer problems.
  • I perform regular user testing so that design decisions can be evidence based.
Some projects I've worked on in the past year include:
  • Designing new tools to allow users to bulk edit data, drastically reducing the amount of time it takes to accomplish a task.
  • Implementing a categorization scheme for customer campaign templates, which resulted in increased adoption of the tool.
  • A UI refresh of our main products, bringing our aesthetic up to modern standards while improving usability. (still in progress)

Previously

I've been in the tech industry since 2011. Prior to ReCollect I spent 5 years as a front-end developer at Daily Burn. I've also worked in UX Design at a large agency in NYC, and led a small startup from concept to launch. Prior to that I attended NYU’s Interactive Telecommunications Program (ITP) where I designed and prototyped this virtual spin bike. Some might say it was the original Peloton. Before all that, I taught spin classes in NYC, and before that (we're getting way back now), I studied Applied Math and Biology in undergrad.

Looking Forward

I see Basecamp as my next big move. The place I stay for… who knows, maybe forever? I don’t like job hopping much. I want a great company with great people, great ideas, and great benefits, without all the politics and layers of red-tape that come with larger organizations. Basecamp is that company. I've wanted to work for you ever since reading Shape Up last year. You “put your money where your mouth is.” You’re opinionated, you’re thought leaders, and people love you. You've launched a new product (hey hey!), you’re growing, and I’d like to grow with you.

I looked at your team and thought - “there’s not a lot of diversity here, I wonder if I should even apply.” But, you called yourselves out it, which shows integrity and self-awareness. That's the kind of company and people I want to work with.

Why Me?

I want a role that requires me to use my entire skillset: design, code, leadership, collaboration. I want to take the best of everything I’ve ever done in my career and combine it all to do my best work yet. I'm loyal, dedicated, and humble. I love a challenge. I go above and beyond. People like working with me. I share your values. I respect and admire your people and your work, and I know we could do great work together.

I’m Remote

I’ve spent the majority of my career working remotely. First as one of only a handful of remote employees for a primarily co-located company, Daily Burn, and now at a fully distributed company. I get it. I love it. I'm great at it. I love my work. I strive for greatness in my career. I don't live to work; I work to live, and remote is the best environment for me to do that.

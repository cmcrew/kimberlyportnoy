Why Basecamp?

Ever since reading Shape Up last year, I’ve wanted to work for Basecamp. I've never really used your product, so I’m not a diehard Basecamp, the product fan (yet). I am a big fan of Basecamp, the company. I don’t think there is one “right” process in product development, but I do know there are lots of bad ones! I love that you have a system, and that you’ve nailed it down so well you wrote a book on it.

I admire your straightforward approach to management, compensation, and communication overall. You seem to care just as much about how you work as you do about what you build, and I think that’s really cool. It's clear you know a thing or two about design. (My tired eyes appreciated the large font size you chose for your employee handbook as I was reading it late at night.)

Where I Want To Go

I want to take the best of everything I’ve ever done in my career and combine it all to do my best work yet, at Basecamp. I want to create great products with great people. In ten years, I'd like to rise through the ranks from Senior Designer to Principle. I've taught yoga and CrossFit in the past, and I'd like to apply those leadership and mentoring skills to my design career without having to give up working on real projects. I want to learn new skills and share what I know. I want to grow personally and professionally, and I want to do it at Basecamp!

Why Me?

I looked at your team and thought - “there’s not a lot of diversity here, I wonder if I should even apply”. But, you called yourselves out on your lack of diversity, which shows integrity. I love a challenge. I'm used to being one of only a few women in a primarily male-dominated "office". People like working with me. I respect and admire your people and your work, and I know we could do great work together.
